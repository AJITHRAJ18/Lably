Medical Report Analysis in Plain English

Medical report analysis helps you understand what your lab results mean before your doctor appointment. Lab in English turns difficult lab language into clear, calm, and practical explanations you can use to have better conversations with healthcare professionals.

If you want a shorter overview instead, see our medical report summary.

What this medical report analysis covers

  • Biomarkers and what each marker usually indicates.
  • Reference ranges (and why values can differ by lab).
  • Patterns and trends across multiple tests over time.
  • Meaningful next questions to ask your clinician.

Why plain-English analysis matters

Most lab reports are built for clinical workflows, not for patients. Plain-English analysis can reduce confusion and help you feel prepared, less anxious, and more confident about discussing results.

How to read your results (simple steps)

  • Check units and reference ranges first, because they determine how a number is interpreted.
  • Compare with prior tests if available (a trend is often more informative than one result).
  • Look for patterns across related biomarkers, instead of focusing on a single value.
  • Prepare questions that connect results to your symptoms, medications, and medical history.

Common clinician follow-ups

After reviewing your medical report analysis, your clinician may discuss significance, possible causes, next tests, and a plan. Lab in English focuses on helping you understand what to ask.
