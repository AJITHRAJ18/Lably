## Link-building strategy for Lab in English

- **Target audience & link sources**
  - Health and wellness blogs that write about diagnostics, lab tests, and preventive care.
  - Patient communities and forums that help people interpret medical information.
  - Clinician-focused newsletters or blogs discussing patient communication and health literacy.
  - Startup/product directories and SaaS/AI tool lists relevant to healthcare and personal health.

- **Core tactics**
  - **Educational blog content** on your own site:
    - Publish 6–10 high-quality guides (e.g., “How to read a CBC report”, “Common thyroid lab markers explained”).
    - Make each article genuinely useful and link-worthy, with clear examples and visuals.
  - **Guest posts & collaborations**:
    - Reach out to 20–30 relevant blogs and newsletters with topic pitches that reference lab literacy.
    - Offer to write a detailed explainer article or case study; include 1–2 natural links back to Lab in English.
  - **Resource listings & directories**:
    - Submit Lably to healthcare-related directories, AI tool lists, and product hunt-style sites.
    - Focus on sites that have real traffic and editorial oversight, not generic link farms.
  - **Partnerships with small clinics or telehealth providers**:
    - Offer Lab in English as a value-add resource for their patients.
    - Ask for a “Recommended tools” or “Patient resources” page that links to your site.
  - **Support and FAQ linking**:
    - If you participate in patient/doctor communities (Reddit, specialist forums, Q&A sites), provide helpful answers and occasionally link to relevant guides on your site where appropriate (avoid spam).

- **Prioritization (first 60–90 days)**
  - Weeks 1–3:
    - Finalize homepage messaging and on-page SEO (done in this project).
    - Publish at least 3–4 cornerstone articles on your own domain.
  - Weeks 3–6:
    - Build a list of 50–80 relevant blogs/newsletters/directories with contact details.
    - Send 10–15 personalized outreach emails per week with specific topic ideas.
  - Weeks 6–12:
    - Double down on channels that respond (guest posts, podcast appearances, or newsletter mentions).
    - Continue publishing 1 high-quality article every 1–2 weeks.

- **Quality guidelines**
  - Prioritize **relevance and authority** over sheer number of links.
  - Avoid buying links, PBNs, or mass-submission link schemes; they pose long-term risk.
  - Aim for contextual links within informative content, not just logo walls or footers.

- **Measurement & tracking**
  - Maintain a simple spreadsheet with:
    - Site name, URL, contact person, email, status (pitched/accepted/published), and date.
    - URL of each secured backlink and its anchor text.
  - Use Google Search Console to monitor:
    - New referring domains over time.
    - Queries where “Lab in English”, “lab results in plain English”, and similar phrases start to appear.
  - Review performance monthly and refine:
    - Double down on content formats and partner types that generate both traffic and signups.

## Search Console rollout checklist (for target keywords)

- Add and verify the `https://labinenglish.com/` property in Google Search Console.
- Submit this sitemap URL: `https://labinenglish.com/sitemap.xml`.
- Use URL Inspection and request indexing for:
  - `https://labinenglish.com/`
  - `https://labinenglish.com/medical-report-analysis.html`
- In Performance report, add a query filter and track:
  - `medical report analysis`
- Track weekly: impressions, average position, CTR, and clicks.
